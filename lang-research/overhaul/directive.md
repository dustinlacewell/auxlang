# Research Directive

## Mission

Build a complete, exhaustive MAP of the poly expansion problem space WITHOUT proposing solutions. The map must be so clear that anyone looking at it immediately understands the entire range and subtlety of the design challenge.

## Method

1. **One file at a time** - Read each source file, extract concepts, update research files
2. **Incremental synthesis** - Don't read everything then dump. Build understanding piece by piece.
3. **Track leads** - When encountering something that needs investigation but isn't current focus, log it
4. **Categorize distinctly** - Identify ontological categories, not implementation buckets
5. **No solutions yet** - This is problem mapping, not problem solving

## Required Outputs

The research must enumerate:

- All systems involved and their responsibilities
- All ontologies (what kinds of things exist)
- All device types and their distinct behavioral categories
- The actual domain problem in terms of graph processing and phasing
- What information is needed at each step
- What needs what information, where and when
- All variations of requirements

## Success Criteria

The final map should enable:
- Fresh reimagining unconstrained by previous decisions
- Global synthesis of ALL needs into a unified view
- Clear identification of where current abstractions fail
- Foundation for throwing 50 agents at a well-defined problem

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Chasing one thread at a time without synthesis
- Reading all code then producing one big dump
- Proposing solutions before the problem is fully mapped
- Letting previous implementation decisions constrain thinking
- Conflating "how it works now" with "what it needs to do"

## Research Files

As categories emerge, create files for:
- Ontological categories discovered
- Open questions that need answers
- Leads to investigate later
- Information flow requirements
- Phase/timing requirements
- Device behavior taxonomy
- Graph structure requirements
